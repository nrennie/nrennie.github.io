Education

PhD Statistics and Operational Research, Lancaster University

I'm currently undertaking a PhD at STOR-i CDT in Lancaster, due to finish in September 2021. My PhD is entitled "Data-Driven Alerts in Revenue Management", and is supervised by Catherine Cleophas (Christian-Albrechts Universität zu Kiel), Adam Sykulski (Lancaster University), and Florian Dost (University of Manchester). Revenue management maximizes revenue by optimizing prices or the offered set of fare classes based on forecasted demand. Data-driven alerts rely on pattern recognition and are already common in the domain of credit card fraud detection. A similar principle could apply in airline revenue management, detecting booking behaviour that deviates significantly from the automated forecasts' predictions. The resulting alerts could highlight relevant targets for interventions to improve the automated system. The objective of the project is to develop methods to highlight deviations between real-world observations and system expectations to assist analysts in targeting manual interventions to the quasi-automated revenue management systems.

In August 2020, I was awarded the Nick Smith Prize for Research Excellence.

PhD Research
My PhD is entitled "Data-driven Alerts in Revenue Management", and seeks to analyse and support the role of analysts within revenue management (RM) systems. RM solves an optimises a set of offers for perishable products, with the objective being to maximise revenue. This process relies heavily on accurate forecasts of demand. Unexpected events can cause outlier demand, which RM systems need to respond to in order to remian optimal. In this case, most RM systems allow analysts to manually override the forecast and make an adjustment. However, outlier demand can be difficult to spot by eye since humans are fallible, and often see patterns that don't exist. My PhD research explores statistical methods for automatically identifying outlier demand, and therefore situations, which would benefit from analyst adjustment.

Birthplaces of Artists

This dataset consists of the metadata for around 70,000 artworks that Tate owns or jointly owns with the National Galleries of Scotland as part of ARTIST ROOMS. Metadata for around 3,500 associated artists is also included. Each point represents the birthplace of an artist whose work is owned by Tate, with the size and colour of the point representing the number of their artworks that are owned by Tate.

Transit-infrastructure Costs

Why do transit-infrastructure projects in New York cost 20 times more on a per kilometer basis than in Seoul? This question has been investigated across hundreds of transit projects from around the world. This dataset spans more than 50 countries and totals more than 11,000 km of urban rail built since the late 1990s. The plot shows the construction timelines for the most expensive transit-infrastructure projects in each country. The United States is a clear outlier.
